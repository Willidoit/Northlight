Questions, answered

Yes — completely. During the public beta, Northlight is free to download and use with no limits. No credit card, no trial countdown, no account signup. We want photographers to actually use it before we ask for anything.

When we launch publicly, Northlight will be $50 one-time or $5/month. Beta users who download during this period will be notified in advance and offered an early-adopter discount on the one-time license. Nothing changes automatically — you won't be charged without choosing to.

If you'll use Northlight for more than a year, the $50 one-time purchase pays for itself in ten months versus the monthly plan. If you'd rather try it at a lower initial commitment, $5/month is there. Either way, your edits and catalog are always yours to keep — canceling the monthly plan never locks you out of your files.

Northlight can import your Lightroom catalog — including star ratings, color labels, collections, and folder structure. Basic edits and metadata transfer cleanly. Complex Lightroom-specific adjustments are translated to their closest equivalent. We're actively expanding compatibility with every beta update.

Northlight requires macOS 13 Ventura or later. It runs natively on Apple Silicon (M1, M2, M3, and M4 series) and is also fully compatible with Intel Macs. Performance is significantly faster on Apple Silicon — RAW decoding and export can be 3–5× quicker than on comparable Intel hardware.

Your photos, edits, and catalog never leave your Mac. We don't have servers that touch your creative work. The only network request Northlight makes is an optional anonymous crash report (opt-in) and a license check on purchase. That's it. No telemetry, no AI training pipeline, no ad data.
